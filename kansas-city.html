Getting to KC

Fly into Kansas City International (MCI) — the new single terminal opened in 2023 and it's an easy airport. Roughly 3h15m nonstop from the NYC area.

Fly

MCI Airport

Nonstops from EWR, LGA and JFK on United, Delta, American and Southwest. MCI sits ~25 min northwest of downtown; rideshare downtown runs about $35–50. Book early — quarterfinal weekend demand will be brutal.

Airport → City

ConnectKC26

KC2026's official transit program runs direct connections between the airport, downtown, the Fan Festival and the stadium during the tournament. Check kansascityfwc26.com for current routes and passes.

In Town

KC Streetcar

Completely free, runs the main downtown spine: River Market → Power & Light → Union Station (Fan Fest) → Country Club Plaza on the extension. You won't need a car if you stay along this line.

Getting to Arrowhead

The one thing to plan carefully: there is no rail to the stadium. Arrowhead sits in the Truman Sports Complex, ~15 min east of downtown off I-70.

Best Option

Stadium Direct Bus

ConnectKC26 Stadium Direct shuttles run from downtown hubs straight to the gates on match day. Reserve a pass in advance — routes have been selling out during the group stage.

Rideshare

Uber / Lyft

Fine going in, painful coming out. Designated pickup zones are a long walk and surge pricing after a quarterfinal will be extreme. Consider dropping a pin at Kauffman Stadium side or walking out to Blue Ridge Cutoff.

Driving

Pre-Booked Parking

Parking must be pre-purchased — no cash lots on the day. Arrive 3+ hours early; I-70 eastbound will crawl from mid-afternoon. Tailgating culture is elite here, so early isn't a punishment.

Fan Festival & Food

Free

FIFA Fan Festival

South lawn of the National WWI Museum & Memorial, overlooking the skyline — every match on big screens, concerts, food vendors. Free, but reserve a digital pass in advance. Right next to Union Station streetcar stop.

Non-Negotiable

KC BBQ

Joe's KC (the gas station original) for the Z-Man, Arthur Bryant's near the stadium for the old-school pilgrimage, Q39 for burnt ends done modern, Gates for the "HI, MAY I HELP YOU?" experience.

Pre-Match

Boulevard & Beyond

Boulevard Brewing tours in the Crossroads, jazz at the Green Lady Lounge, and if you have a free morning — the WWI Museum itself is genuinely world-class.

Match Day Plan

11:00 AM

BBQ Lunch

Beat the lines at Joe's KC or Q39. You do not want a heavy meal at 6 PM in July heat.

1:00 PM

Fan Festival

Catch the early quarterfinal on the big screens at the WWI Museum lawn. Hydrate — KC in July runs 90°F+ and humid.

4:30 PM

Head East

Board the Stadium Direct bus or start the drive. Gates typically open ~3 hours before kickoff for knockout matches.

5:30 PM

Arrowhead Lots

Soak in the tailgate scene — the sports complex parking lots on a big match day are an event in themselves.

8:00 PM

Kickoff — Match 100

A World Cup semifinal spot on the line, in a stadium that once hit 142.2 decibels. Ears optional.

11:00 PM+

The Exit

Expect 60–90 min to clear the complex. Pre-book your ride back or take the bus, then celebrate (or mourn) at Power & Light.
